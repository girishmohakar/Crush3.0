About the Stone Chariot

The Stone Chariot is located inside the Vittala Temple complex in Hampi, Karnataka. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is actually a monumental shrine built in the form of a chariot, dedicated to Garuda (the vehicle of Lord Vishnu).

Fun Facts

  • The Stone Chariot is one of the 3 famous stone chariots in India (Hampi, Konark, and Mahabalipuram).
  • It is featured on the ₹50 Indian currency note (new design series).
  • The chariot is carved out of granite blocks, so precisely joined that it looks like a single piece.
  • Earlier, the wheels of the chariot could rotate, but they have been cemented now to protect the structure.
  • The carvings depict mythological stories, celestial beings, and motifs of Vijayanagara art.
  • It serves as a symbol of Karnataka's rich heritage and Indian temple architecture.

History

The Stone Chariot was built during the reign of King Krishnadevaraya (16th century) of the Vijayanagara Empire. It was inspired by the Konark Sun Temple Chariot (Odisha), but is smaller and more intricate.

The Vijayanagara Empire was one of the most powerful South Indian kingdoms, known for its magnificent architecture and rich cultural heritage. The Stone Chariot represents the pinnacle of Vijayanagara architectural excellence.

The chariot is dedicated to Garuda, the divine mount of Lord Vishnu. The structure was originally designed as a functional shrine where devotional activities took place. The intricate carvings showcase the artistic mastery of medieval Indian craftsmen.

The Vittala Temple complex, where the Stone Chariot stands, was never completed due to the fall of the Vijayanagara Empire in 1565. Despite being incomplete, it remains one of the most photographed monuments in India and a testament to the empire's grandeur.

Visiting Information

The Stone Chariot is located in the Vittala Temple complex, Hampi, Karnataka. Hampi is well-connected by road, rail, and air, making it accessible for tourists from across India and around the world.

By Train: Hospet Junction is the nearest railway station (13 km away) with good connectivity to major cities like Bengaluru, Mumbai, Chennai, and Hyderabad.

By Air: Hubli Airport is the nearest airport (around 150 km away). Bengaluru Airport (350 km) offers better connectivity for international travelers.

By Road: Regular bus services from Hospet, Bengaluru, Goa, and other major cities. State transport and private buses regularly operate to Hampi.

Temple Timings: Vittala Temple complex is open from 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM. The complex is closed on Fridays for maintenance.

Entry Fee: ₹40 for Indians, ₹600 for foreign nationals. Children below 15 years enter free of charge.

Accommodation: Hospet offers various hotels and lodges. Hampi has budget guesthouses and heritage resorts for all types of travelers.

Best Time to Visit: October to February offers pleasant weather. Avoid summer months (March-May) due to extreme heat.
