About the Temple

The Gajanan Maharaj Temple in Shegaon is one of the most revered spiritual places in Maharashtra. Devotees from across the country visit to seek blessings of Shri Gajanan Maharaj, a saint known for his miracles and divine grace.

  • The temple is known for its cleanliness and discipline.
  • The Anand Sagar project nearby is a major tourist attraction.
  • Millions of devotees visit Shegaon every year during festivals.

History

The temple was established in the late 19th century at the site where Shri Gajanan Maharaj took Samadhi. Over the years, it has become a hub of devotion, culture, and service, attracting lakhs of devotees annually.

The Sansthan at Shegaon manages several social and educational institutions inspired by the teachings of Shri Gajanan Maharaj, carrying forward his message of simplicity, service, and devotion.

Shri Gajanan Maharaj appeared in Shegaon in February 1878 and lived there until his Samadhi in 1910. During his 32 years of divine presence, he performed countless miracles and blessed thousands of devotees.

The original temple structure was simple, but over the decades it has been expanded and beautified while maintaining its spiritual sanctity. The Anand Sagar project, initiated in the 1990s, has transformed Shegaon into a major pilgrimage and tourist destination.

Visiting Information

Shegaon is well-connected by road and rail. The temple complex includes accommodation facilities, Prasad distribution, and an Anand Sagar project developed for devotees and tourists.

By Train: Shegaon Railway Station is well connected to major cities. Regular trains from Mumbai, Pune, Delhi, and other major cities halt here.

By Road: State transport and private buses regularly ply to Shegaon. The temple is about 85 km from Akola and 55 km from Buldhana.

Accommodation: The Sansthan provides various accommodation options including air-conditioned rooms, dormitories, and budget-friendly lodging for all types of pilgrims.

Best Time to Visit: October to March offers pleasant weather. Avoid monsoon season for outdoor activities at Anand Sagar.

Temple Timings: 4:30 AM to 10:00 PM daily. Special darshan timings during festivals and important occasions.
