About Ajanta & Ellora Caves

The Ajanta and Ellora Caves, located near Aurangabad in Maharashtra, are among India's most remarkable historical and architectural marvels. These UNESCO World Heritage Sites represent Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain rock-cut cave temples and monasteries carved into the Sahyadri hills.

Fun Facts

  • Ajanta Caves were discovered in 1819 by British officer Captain John Smith during a hunting expedition.
  • Ellora's Kailasa Temple (Cave 16) is one of the world's largest monolithic structures, carved from a single rock.
  • Ajanta's frescoes are some of the earliest surviving examples of Indian classical art, dating back to 2nd century BCE.
  • Ellora uniquely represents three religions - Buddhism, Hinduism, and Jainism - in a single site.
  • Both sites were designated UNESCO World Heritage Sites in 1983.
  • Ajanta murals inspired later Indian art forms, including Mughal and Deccan miniature paintings.

History

The Ajanta Caves were built between 200 BCE and 480 CE as Buddhist monasteries, prayer halls, and study centers. Ellora Caves were constructed later between 6th and 10th centuries CE under the patronage of Rashtrakuta and Kalachuri dynasties.

Ajanta consists of 29 caves carved into a horseshoe-shaped cliff, famous for their exquisite murals depicting scenes from Jataka tales (stories of Buddha's past lives). These paintings showcase mastery in perspective, shading, and emotional expression.

Ellora comprises 34 caves representing three religions: 12 Buddhist caves (1-12), 17 Hindu caves (13-29), and 5 Jain caves (30-34). The most spectacular is Cave 16, the Kailasa Temple, representing Mount Kailash, Lord Shiva's abode.

Unlike Ajanta which was abandoned and forgotten for centuries, Ellora remained continuously known. These sites together demonstrate the religious harmony and artistic excellence of ancient and medieval India, attracting millions of visitors annually.

Visiting Information

Both Ajanta and Ellora Caves are located near Aurangabad, Maharashtra. Ajanta is about 100 km from Aurangabad, while Ellora is just 30 km away. Both sites are well-connected by road, rail, and air for tourists from across India and around the world.

By Train: Aurangabad railway station connects with major cities like Mumbai, Pune, Hyderabad, and Delhi. From the station, buses and taxis are available to both cave sites.

By Air: Aurangabad Airport (IXU) is 30 km from Ellora and 100 km from Ajanta. The airport has good connectivity to major Indian cities including Mumbai, Delhi, and Hyderabad.

By Road: Regular bus services from Aurangabad, Pune, Nashik, and Mumbai. State transport and private buses operate frequently to both destinations.

Timings: Ajanta: 9:00 AM - 5:30 PM (Closed Mondays); Ellora: 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM (Closed Tuesdays)

Entry Fee: Indian citizens: ₹40; Foreign nationals: ₹600; Children below 15 years: Free entry

Accommodation: Aurangabad offers various hotels from budget to luxury. Guided tours and light & sound shows are available at Ellora.

Best Time to Visit: November to March offers pleasant weather. Monsoon season (July-September) enhances the landscape with lush greenery.
