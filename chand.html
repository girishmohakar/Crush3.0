About Chand Baori

Chand Baori is a magnificent stepwell located in the village of Abhaneri, near Jaipur, Rajasthan. Built over a thousand years ago, it is considered one of the deepest and largest stepwells in the world, featuring 3,500 narrow steps arranged in perfect symmetry descending 13 stories deep.

Fun Facts

  • Chand Baori consists of 3,500 narrow steps arranged in perfect symmetry, making it one of the deepest stepwells in India.
  • The stepwell plunges about 13 stories deep (approximately 100 feet/30 meters) into the ground.
  • Temperature at the bottom is often 5-6°C cooler than the surface, making it a natural air-conditioner.
  • Featured in Hollywood movies including "The Dark Knight Rises" as the prison pit scene.
  • Built during the 9th century CE by King Chanda of the Nikumbh dynasty, from whom it derives its name.
  • The stepwell is shaped like an inverted pyramid and covers an area of around 50 x 50 meters.

History

Chand Baori was built during the reign of King Chanda of the Nikumbh dynasty in the 9th century CE. This magnificent stepwell was constructed as an ingenious water-harvesting system in the arid region of Rajasthan to store rainwater and groundwater, ensuring a year-round water supply for the community.

The stepwell served both practical and cultural functions throughout history. Practically, it provided drinking water, irrigation support, and a cool resting place during the scorching Rajasthan heat. Culturally, it became a sacred space associated with rituals, festivals, and temple activities.

Located opposite the Harshat Mata Temple, dedicated to the Goddess of Joy and Happiness, Chand Baori reflects the spiritual connection where water and divinity were closely linked in ancient Indian culture. During the medieval period, the stepwell also acted as a gathering site for villagers, especially in the summer months.

The stepwell represents the blend of engineering skill and artistic vision of medieval Indian architects. Its precise geometry and crisscross pattern of steps forming a visually striking maze demonstrate the advanced mathematical and architectural knowledge of ancient India, making it a testament to sustainable water management practices.

Visiting Information

Chand Baori is located in Abhaneri village, about 95 km from Jaipur, Rajasthan. The stepwell is easily accessible by road, rail, and air, making it a popular destination for tourists exploring Rajasthan's architectural heritage. Entry is free, and the site is open daily from 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

By Air: Nearest airport is Jaipur International Airport, located 100 km away. The airport has good connectivity to major Indian cities including Mumbai, Delhi, and Bangalore.

By Train: Nearest railway station is Bandikui Junction, just 8 km away. Jaipur railway station (95 km) is also well-connected to major cities across India.

By Road: Easily accessible by car or bus from Jaipur, Agra, and Delhi. It's part of the popular Golden Triangle route, with regular bus services available.

Timings: Open daily from 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Entry Fee: Free of cost (though guides may charge for tours).

Nearby Attractions: Harshat Mata Temple (right next to Chand Baori), Jaipur City (Amber Fort, Hawa Mahal, City Palace), Bhangarh Fort, and Sariska National Park.

Best Time to Visit: October to March offers pleasant weather. Summers can be extremely hot, so early morning or late afternoon visits are recommended.

Facilities: Basic facilities like guides, shops, and rest areas are available. Photography is allowed, making it a popular destination for architectural enthusiasts and filmmakers.
