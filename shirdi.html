About the Temple

The Shirdi Sai Baba Temple, located in Shirdi, Ahmednagar district, Maharashtra, is one of the most famous pilgrimage sites in India. It is dedicated to Sai Baba of Shirdi, a saint revered by both Hindus and Muslims for his teachings of love, equality, and service to humanity.

History

Sai Baba came to Shirdi in 1858, initially living under a neem tree, later settling in a mosque (which he named Dwarkamai). He lived a simple life, healing the sick, helping the poor, and spreading messages of faith (Shraddha) and patience (Saburi).

Sai Baba was born around 1838 (birth details are debated, as he never revealed much about his early life). He came to Shirdi in 1858 and lived there until his Samadhi in 1918, a period of 60 years of divine presence.

In 1918, Sai Baba took Samadhi (left his mortal body). His followers built the shrine over his tomb. Over time, Shirdi developed into a large pilgrimage town with the temple as its heart.

Sai Baba never claimed to belong to any particular religion and welcomed people of all faiths. His famous saying "Sabka Malik Ek" (One God governs all) continues to inspire millions of devotees who visit Shirdi seeking his blessings.

Visiting Information

Shirdi is located in Ahmednagar district, Maharashtra, about 250 km from Mumbai, 90 km from Nashik, and 120 km from Aurangabad. The temple is well-connected by road, rail, and air.

By Train: Sainagar Shirdi Railway Station is well connected to Mumbai, Pune, Nagpur, Chennai, and other major cities across India.

By Air: Shirdi Airport (SAG) is about 15 km away with regular flights from major cities.

By Road: Excellent bus services from Mumbai, Pune, Nashik, and Aurangabad. State transport and private buses regularly ply to Shirdi.

Temple Timings: Temple opens at 4:00 AM with Kakada Aarti and closes around 11:15 PM after Shej Aarti.

Major Aartis: Kakada Aarti (4:30 AM), Madhyan Aarti (12:00 PM), Dhoop Aarti (Sunset), and Shej Aarti (10:30 PM). Advance booking required for participation.

Accommodation: The Shri Saibaba Sansthan Trust provides affordable rooms, and many hotels are available nearby for all budgets.

Best Time to Visit: October to March offers pleasant weather. Avoid peak summer months.
